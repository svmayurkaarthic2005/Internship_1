SIS WORKFLOW GUIDE FOR ISD AND NISD APPLICATIONS

=== ISD WORKFLOW (Involving Sub-Division) ===

Step 1: Application Submission
- Applications can be submitted through CSC centers, Sub-Registrar offices, or directly by citizens
- Application receives a unique tracking number (format: ISD/DISTRICT_CODE/YEAR/SEQUENCE)
- All required documents must be uploaded at the time of submission

Step 2: SIS Officer Assignment
- Application is automatically assigned to the SIS officer based on jurisdiction (District/Taluk/Town/Ward/Block)
- Officer receives notification within 24 hours
- Application appears in officer's pending queue

Step 3: SIS Field Visit
- Officer must schedule field visit within 15 working days from application submission
- Field visit is mandatory for all ISD applications
- Officer verifies the survey number, boundaries, area measurements, and checks for encroachments
- Officer takes photographs and GPS coordinates during the visit
- Officer prepares preliminary field notes and sketch

Step 4: Sketch Preparation by Survey Department (SD)
- After field verification, SIS officer submits findings to Survey Department
- SD prepares detailed survey sketch with new sub-division numbers
- SD verifies area calculations: sum of all sub-divisions must equal original survey area
- Sub-division numbering follows pattern: 145 → 145/1A, 145/1B, 145/2A, etc.
- SD handoff typically takes 7-10 working days

Step 5: Deputy Inspector Surveyor (DIS) Approval
- DIS reviews the field report and survey sketch
- DIS verifies compliance with survey rules and area calculations
- DIS approves or rejects with reasons
- Approval timeline: 5 working days

Step 6: Digital Sign-off by Tahsildar
- Approved applications move to Tahsildar for final digital signature
- Tahsildar applies Digital Signature Certificate (DSC)
- DSC is mandatory for all patta transfers
- Timeline: 3 working days

Step 7: Patta Transfer Order Generation
- System generates patta transfer order with new sub-division details
- Order includes previous owner name, new owner name, survey numbers, area details
- Citizen can download patta transfer order from portal
- Land records are updated in the revenue system

Total Timeline: Approximately 30-35 working days from submission to completion

=== NISD WORKFLOW (Not Involving Sub-Division) ===

Step 1: Application Submission
- Application submitted through CSC, Sub-Registrar, or citizen portal
- Application number format: NISD/DISTRICT_CODE/YEAR/SEQUENCE
- No new sub-divisions are created, only ownership transfer

Step 2: SIS Officer Verification
- SIS officer verifies documents: Sale Deed, EC (Encumbrance Certificate), Photo ID
- Officer checks if survey number exists and area matches records
- Officer verifies there are no pending disputes or litigation
- No field visit required unless specific issues are flagged
- Verification timeline: 5 working days

Step 3: Deputy Tahsildar Review
- Deputy Tahsildar reviews SIS officer's verification report
- Checks for any revenue arrears or pending taxes
- Confirms sale deed registration details
- Timeline: 3 working days

Step 4: Digital Signature Certificate (DSC) Application
- Tahsildar applies DSC to patta transfer order
- DSC validates the authenticity of the transfer
- Timeline: 2 working days

Step 5: Patta Transfer Completion
- Patta ownership is transferred to new owner
- Old owner's name is removed from records
- New owner's name is updated in land records system

Step 6: Land Records Update
- Revenue system is updated with new ownership details
- Patta number remains same, only owner changes
- Property tax records are updated

Step 7: Citizen Download
- Citizen receives notification on mobile/email
- Citizen can download updated patta from portal
- Physical copy can be collected from Tahsildar office

Total Timeline: Approximately 15-20 working days from submission to completion

=== 15 WORKING DAY FIELD VISIT DEADLINE RULE ===

- For ISD applications, field visit MUST be scheduled within 15 working days of application submission
- If field visit is not completed within 15 days, application is marked as "overdue"
- Overdue applications are flagged in officer's dashboard with red indicator
- Officers receive escalation notifications for overdue applications
- Overdue applications affect officer's performance metrics
- Officers must provide justification for delays beyond 15 days

=== REQUIRED DOCUMENTS FOR ISD ===

1. Sale Deed
   - Must be registered with Sub-Registrar
   - Original scan copy required
   - Must contain buyer and seller details, survey number, area

2. Encumbrance Certificate (EC)
   - Shows clear title for past 13 years
   - Must be obtained from Sub-Registrar office
   - Valid for 3 months from issue date

3. Survey Sketch
   - Existing survey sketch from revenue records
   - Shows current survey number and boundaries
   - Can be obtained from Tahsildar office

4. Photo ID
   - Aadhaar card (mandatory)
   - Additional ID: Driving License, Voter ID, Passport (any one)
   - Both buyer and seller photo IDs required

5. Photographs
   - Current photographs of the property
   - Boundary stone photographs (if applicable)
   - Minimum 4 photographs from different angles

=== REQUIRED DOCUMENTS FOR NISD ===

1. Sale Deed
   - Registered with Sub-Registrar
   - Original scan copy required
   - Must match survey number in application

2. Encumbrance Certificate (EC)
   - Clear title for past 13 years
   - Valid for 3 months from issue date

3. Photo ID
   - Aadhaar card of buyer and seller
   - Additional ID proof (any one government ID)

4. Patta Copy
   - Current patta copy showing previous owner
   - Can be obtained from Tahsildar office or online portal

=== REJECTION REASONS AND RESUBMISSION PROCESS ===

Common Rejection Reasons:

1. Incomplete Documents
   - Missing required documents (Sale Deed, EC, IDs)
   - Invalid or expired documents
   - Documents not properly attested

2. Area Mismatch
   - Area in Sale Deed does not match survey records
   - Sub-division area calculations incorrect
   - Total area of sub-divisions does not equal original survey area

3. Encroachment Issues
   - Encroachment detected during field visit
   - Boundary disputes with neighboring properties
   - Unauthorized construction on land

4. Litigation Flag
   - Ongoing court cases related to the property
   - Disputed ownership claims
   - Stay orders from court

5. Revenue Arrears
   - Pending property taxes
   - Outstanding dues on the survey number
   - Non-payment of water/electricity charges linked to property

6. Invalid Survey Number
   - Survey number does not exist in records
   - Survey number belongs to different jurisdiction
   - Survey number already sub-divided

Resubmission Process:

- Officer provides detailed rejection reason in system
- Applicant receives notification with rejection reason
- Applicant must rectify issues and resubmit application
- Resubmitted applications get priority in queue
- Same application number is retained with suffix (e.g., ISD/01/2024/001-R1)
- Maximum 3 resubmissions allowed
- After 3 rejections, applicant must file fresh application

Timeline for Resubmission:
- Applicant has 30 days to resubmit after rejection
- If not resubmitted within 30 days, application is auto-closed
- Closed applications can be reopened with written request to Tahsildar

=== ESCALATION PROCESS ===

Level 1 Escalation (15 days overdue):
- Notification to SIS officer and DIS
- Application marked with yellow flag

Level 2 Escalation (30 days overdue):
- Notification to Tahsildar
- Application marked with red flag
- Officer must provide written explanation

Level 3 Escalation (45 days overdue):
- Notification to District Revenue Officer (DRO)
- Officer performance review triggered
- Possible reassignment of application
